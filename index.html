Gentleman's Cryptogram

Take a secret message and embed it in a letter,

using the timeless Caesar Cipher method

Dear Spencer,
I was overjoyed to receive your letter back last summer and also to hear of your project to revive the lost tradition of the inked note. However, you being the person that you are and our friendship being what it is, I declared when I received your card that I must respond in a manner befitting of the correspondence. It was therefore my solemn task to devise a method of communication that would be appropriate given our context. Now, there were some obvious and attractive choices that presented themselves, but it seems that all contemporary use would prove to be either entirely too time consuming on my end, or wholly impractical on yours. Thankfully though, I revived this notion when I was considering new challenging projects. It is with this that I quietly embarked on a program that would borrow an archaic method of encipherment and make it relevant once again. You are receiving this letter from me now because I have progressed to the point where I am now ready to valiantly share with you the present fruits of my labor. So while I understand some underlying frustration in my egregiously delayed response, I hope the manner and care in which I have written back will make up for my quiescence. Thank you Spencer for being my light of inspiration for this project. I can only hope to be such a source for you in the future. 
With levity and merriment, Robert
